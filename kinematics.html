Kinematics: Motion & Movement

Understanding Motion in One and Two Dimensions

What is Kinematics?

Kinematics is the branch of physics that describes the motion of objects without considering the forces that cause the motion. It deals with displacement, velocity, acceleration, and time.

📊 Speed

Speed is a scalar quantity measuring how fast an object moves.

→ Velocity

A vector quantity that includes speed and direction. Speed measures total distance, whereas velocity measures displacement (e.g., 50 km/h north).

Formula: Velocity = Distance ÷ Time

Velocity Formula Triangle

📏 Distance

A scalar quantity representing the total length of the path covered by an object between two points, regardless of direction.

Formula: Distance = Speed × Time

⏱️ Time

The fundamental scalar quantity used to measure the duration of events and quantify changes in an object's position, velocity, and acceleration.

Formula: Time = Distance ÷ Speed

⚡ Acceleration

The rate at which an object's velocity changes, measuring how quickly it speeds up, slows down, or changes direction.

Kinematic Variables

  • v = final velocity
  • u = initial velocity
  • t = total time taken
  • a = acceleration

Visual Learning: Motion Diagrams

Uniform Motion

Constant velocity - object moves the same distance in equal time intervals.

Accelerated Motion

Velocity changes over time - object covers increasing distances in equal time intervals.

Projectile Motion

2D motion under gravity - combines horizontal uniform motion with vertical accelerated motion.
